How to Use This Mortgage Calculator

Our mortgage calculator helps you estimate your monthly mortgage payment and see the impact of different variables on your costs. Here's how to use it:

  1. Enter your home price - The total purchase price of the home.
  2. Set your down payment - Either as a dollar amount or percentage of the home price.
  3. Choose your loan term - Typically 30, 20, or 15 years.
  4. Enter your interest rate - The annual interest rate for your mortgage.
  5. Add property tax and insurance - Annual amounts for property tax and homeowners insurance.
  6. Include HOA fees if applicable - Monthly homeowners association fees, if any.
  7. Consider extra payments - Adding extra to your monthly payment can significantly reduce your loan term and interest paid.
  8. Click "Calculate" - View your monthly payment breakdown and amortization schedule.

Adjust any of these values to see how they affect your monthly payment and long-term costs.

Mortgage Calculator FAQ

What is included in a mortgage payment?

A typical mortgage payment consists of four components, often referred to as PITI:

  • Principal: The amount that goes toward paying off your loan balance.
  • Interest: The cost of borrowing the money, paid to your lender.
  • Taxes: Property taxes collected by your lender and paid to your local government.
  • Insurance: Homeowners insurance premiums to protect your property.

Additionally, you may have PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance) if your down payment is less than 20%, and HOA fees if your property is part of a homeowners association.

How does changing my loan term affect my mortgage?

Shorter loan terms (like 15 years instead of 30 years) typically offer:

  • Lower interest rates
  • Higher monthly payments
  • Significantly less total interest paid over the life of the loan
  • Faster equity building
  • Earlier loan payoff

Longer loan terms offer lower monthly payments but cost more in total interest over time.

How do extra payments affect my mortgage?

Making extra payments toward your principal can:

  • Shorten your loan term significantly
  • Save thousands in interest payments
  • Build equity faster

Even small additional monthly payments can make a big difference over time. Our calculator shows you exactly how much you can save with extra payments.
