Understanding Percentages

Percentages are a fundamental concept in mathematics used to express a number as a fraction of 100. They are widely used in everyday life, from calculating discounts and tips to understanding statistics and financial reports. The word "percent" comes from the Latin "per centum," meaning "by the hundred."

Key Percentage Concepts

  • Calculating a Percentage: To find what percentage one number (part) is of another number (whole), use the formula: (Part / Whole) * 100 = Percentage.
  • Finding a Percentage of a Number: To find a specific percentage of a number, convert the percentage to a decimal (divide by 100) and multiply by the number: (Percentage / 100) * Number = Result.
  • Percentage Change: To calculate the percentage increase or decrease between two values (old and new), use the formula: ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) * 100 = Percentage Change.
  • Discounts and Markups: Percentages are commonly used to calculate price adjustments. A discount reduces the price, while a markup increases it.

Tips for Working with Percentages

  1. Always identify the "whole" or the base amount when calculating percentages.
  2. Convert percentages to decimals or fractions before performing calculations.
  3. Double-check whether you are calculating a percentage of a number, the percentage itself, or the base number.
